question:4 c's of marketing

answer:The 4 Cs of marketing are a customer-oriented alternative to the traditional 4 Ps of marketing (Product, Price, Place, Promotion). The 4 Cs focus on the customer perspective and include: 1. Customer: Understanding and meeting the needs and wants of the target customers. 2. Cost: The total cost that customers incur in acquiring and using the product or service, which includes not only the purchase price but also other costs associated with using the product. 3. Convenience: Making it easy and convenient for customers to purchase and use the product or service, such as providing multiple channels for purchase or ensuring availability. 4. Communication: Engaging in meaningful dialogue with customers to build relationships, establish trust, and provide value through effective communication channels. By considering the 4 Cs, companies can better align their marketing strategies with the needs and preferences of their target customers.
